He was an old man, who lived by himself
in a old cabin, lost to the south
he had no happiness, no family, no wealth
he built with his hands and, cared for his health

rustling river, sparkling leaves, the sounds that he knew
whistling birds, howling wind, the chittering wolves
the fungus grew from things he knew, the cracks of breaking wood,
his empty flask, his broken ax, his coats of wool and fur

as the skies darkened, frost grew on earth,
he knelt at the table, his wick slowly burnt
he raised both his hands and, curled into fists
struck at the daylight, the moments that he missed,
the food that he savored, the people he’d kissed

one day he could feel, no more through the pain
fell onto worn carpet, and started to sing

whistling birds, howling wind, the chittering wolves

his empty flask, his broken ax, his coats of wool and fur
